Meet the Team

Drawing on a background in anticorruption activism, financial services law, and investment banking and on training in mathematics, Zia Haider Rahman is leading a team building a platform that uses network science to increase transparency in governmental and corporate relationships.

He is the Walter Jackson Bate Fellow at the Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study, Harvard University, a fellow at New America, Washington, DC, and a senior fellow at the Bruno Kreisky Forum, in Vienna, and has been appointed a Director’s Visitor at the Institute for Advanced Study, in Princeton. He was born in Bangladesh, grew up in the UK, and was educated at the University of Oxford (Balliol College), the University of Cambridge, and Yale University.

His first novel, In the Light of What We Know, was published to international critical acclaim and won the James Tait Black Prize for Fiction, Britain’s oldest literary prize, previous winners of which include Cormac McCarthy, Nadine Gordimer, Salman Rushdie, and Evelyn Waugh.
